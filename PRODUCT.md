# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary players are the developer colleagues being affectionately parodied in the game. They understand startup software engineering, one another's working styles, and the changing role of AI tooling. The vertical slice is a private team experience rather than a product aimed at a broad commercial audience.

## Product Purpose

Create a polished, replayable single-act deckbuilder about a small software team that is highly capable and responsible for much of its own chaos. Players assemble a squad of three developers, make tactically satisfying decisions through a run, and enjoy recognisable character comedy enhanced by energetic presentation. Success means the tactical play, affectionate parody, and visual polish feel inseparable rather than competing for attention.

## Brand Personality

Affectionate, kinetic, and mischievous. The comedy celebrates the people involved and their self-aware working styles. It can tease startup life, developer habits, and AI-assisted engineering without making communication needs or neurodivergence into punchlines.

## Anti-references

- Corporate SaaS dashboards or productivity software wearing a thin game skin
- Generic medieval deckbuilder parchment, fantasy ornament, or dungeon gloom
- Dark, oppressive presentation similar to Slay the Spire
- Direct imitation of Persona's visual identity
- Mean-spirited workplace satire or bureaucracy as the primary source of humour
- Nostalgic physical-office dressing such as photocopiers, filing cabinets, paper clutter, or cubicle culture as the default visual language
- Literal recreations of productivity software, digital whiteboards, ticket trackers, or chat applications
- AI-generated visual sameness, excessive glass effects, or interchangeable card grids

## Design Principles

1. Tactical clarity comes first. Players should understand the meaningful decision in front of them even when the screen is energetic. Labels stay short and functional; visuals carry flavour instead of eyebrows, descriptive subheadings, and repeated explanation.
2. Character mechanics carry the comedy. Jokes should emerge from how recognisable people play, combine, and create chaos.
3. Celebrate capability and catastrophe together. The team creates impressive results and ridiculous problems, often with the same action.
4. Feel digitally native without imitating work software. Use the motion and visual grammar of collaborative canvases, graphs, code, cursors, and connected systems as ingredients for a game world.
5. Spend spectacle on decisive moments while keeping the tone bright and affectionate. Signature cards, incidents, shipping, and consequences earn visual excess; routine interaction stays quick and pressure never becomes gloomy or cruel.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target a readable, keyboard-accessible interface with strong focus states, sufficient contrast, and no information conveyed by colour alone. Provide reduced-motion behaviour for large transitions, screen shake, and animated effects. Represent colleagues through traits and working styles they are comfortable being teased about; do not use diagnoses or communication needs as status effects or punchlines.
